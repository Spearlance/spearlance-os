# Landing Page CRO

## Overview

Opinionated landing page optimization for service businesses — plumbers, lawyers, dentists, contractors, salons, HVAC, roofing, and any mid-to-high ticket service where the lead is a phone call or form submission, not an e-commerce checkout.

This skill is not for e-commerce. It is not for SaaS. It is for the business where the phone ringing = money.

---

## 1. Above-the-Fold

The first screen either earns the scroll or kills the lead. No second chances.

### Headline

**Rule:** Benefit-first, max 8 words, specific.

| Wrong | Right |
|-------|-------|
| "Quality Service You Can Trust" | "AC Fixed Same Day — Or It's Free" |
| "Your Local Plumbing Experts" | "Burst Pipe Emergency? We're There in 60 Min" |
| "Dedicated to Excellence" | "Dallas Family Law Since 1998 — 400+ Cases Won" |

The headline must answer: **What do you do, for who, and what outcome should I expect?** in under 8 words. If it could apply to any business in any city, rewrite it.

### Sub-headline

Proof and specificity. Follow the pattern: `[City/Region] + [Tenure or Volume] + [Credibility Signal]`

Examples:
- "Serving Austin homeowners since 2009. 2,400+ jobs completed. Google's #1 rated plumber."
- "Licensed criminal defense attorney. 15 years. 95% case dismissal rate."

One sentence. No fluff. If you have a star rating with review count, put it here.

### Primary CTA

**Action verb + outcome. Never a passive label.**

| Wrong | Right |
|-------|-------|
| "Submit" | "Get Your Free Estimate" |
| "Contact Us" | "Schedule an Inspection Today" |
| "Learn More" | "Call Now — We Answer 24/7" |

Rules:
- Must be above the fold — user should not scroll to find it
- High contrast button — tested against background, not just "looks good"
- For mobile: button height minimum 48px, full-width or near full-width
- For urgent services (emergency plumber, HVAC, locksmith): phone number IS the primary CTA

### Trust Badges

Place in the hero section, below the sub-headline or near the CTA. Not the footer.

Priority order:
1. Google rating + star count ("★★★★★ 4.9 · 312 reviews")
2. BBB accreditation (if A+ rated)
3. Industry certifications (NATE for HVAC, AV-rated for attorneys, board certifications for dentists)
4. License number (makes contractors look legit instantly — "Lic. #CGC1519234")
5. Years in business (only if 10+)

Do not use generic badge graphics with no specificity ("100% Satisfaction Guaranteed" without a backing policy is noise).

### Imagery

Real photos of the team, the work, or the location always outperform stock. Always.

| Business Type | What Works |
|---------------|-----------|
| Contractor | Real job photos, team on-site, before/after |
| Dentist | Office interior, real patient smiles (with permission) |
| Lawyer | Headshot of the actual attorney — not stock courtroom |
| HVAC | Tech in branded uniform on the job |
| Salon | Real client work, real stylists |

Stock photo rule: if the image came from Shutterstock, iStock, or Unsplash, it is killing trust for service businesses. Customers have seen those photos on 400 other sites. Replace them.

## 2. Social Proof Architecture

Social proof is not a section. It is a layer — it should appear near every decision point on the page, not consolidated into one "Testimonials" block at the bottom that no one scrolls to.

### Placement Rules

**Near every CTA.** If there is a CTA button, there should be a proof element within 200px of it (stars, review count, a short testimonial).

```
[Hero Section]
  → Headline
  → Sub-headline with review count
  → CTA Button
  → "★★★★★ 4.9 from 312 Google Reviews" ← proof near CTA

[Services Section]
  → Individual service descriptions

[Social Proof Section]
  → Full testimonials with names and specifics

[Second CTA]
  → "Join 2,400+ Homeowners Who Trust Us" ← proof near CTA

[Contact Form]
  → Short testimonial directly above form ← proof near conversion
```

### Google Reviews Widget

Embed a live Google reviews widget (Elfsight, Trustmary, or custom API). Not screenshots. Not manually copied quotes that users cannot verify.

Place it:
- Prominently in a dedicated section (not buried in footer)
- Ideally after the services section — user has intent, now validate it

### Testimonial Strategy

Specific results beat generic praise. Every time.

| Wrong | Right |
|-------|-------|
| "Great service! Highly recommend." | "They fixed our AC in 2 hours on a Sunday. No extra charge for the weekend. Never going anywhere else." |
| "Very professional team." | "Dr. Chen did my veneers in 2 visits. I cried when I saw the result. Worth every penny." |
| "Excellent work." | "They replaced our roof in one day. Zero mess left. Insurance handled everything." |

Testimonial requirements:
- Full name (first + last initial minimum)
- City or neighborhood
- Specific detail about the job or outcome
- Optional: photo of the person (dramatically increases trust)

### Before/After Galleries

Essential for: contractors, landscaping, dentists, cosmetic services, auto detailing, cleaning services, restoration.

Rules:
- Same angle, same lighting
- Label clearly: "Before" / "After"
- Group by service type
- Each image should have a brief caption: "Oak Park kitchen remodel — 4-day project"

If the business does physical transformation work and has no before/after gallery, this is the highest-leverage thing to add.

### Client/Project Count

Specific numbers build trust. Round numbers look made up.

- "2,847 homes served" > "Thousands of homes served"
- "14 years, 1,200+ cases handled" > "Over a decade of experience"
- "412 Google reviews" > "Hundreds of satisfied customers"

If you do not have exact numbers, use honest approximations with a floor: "More than 500 roofs replaced since 2011."

### Media Mentions

"As Seen In" — if available. Local news features, industry publications, awards. Even local TV is legitimate. Logos of media mentions compress trust instantly.

## 3. Objection Handling

Service businesses lose leads to uncertainty. The prospect has three objections and they are always the same three: **How much does it cost? How long will it take? Can I trust you?**

Address them head-on. Do not make the visitor work to find answers.

### FAQ Section

Must address at minimum:

1. **Price** — "What does it cost?" People will not call if they fear sticker shock. Options:
   - "Starting at $X" — anchors expectations
   - "Free estimates — no obligation" — removes risk of calling
   - "We match any licensed competitor's quote" — aggressive but effective

2. **Timeline** — "How fast can you get here / get it done?"
   - Emergency services: response time SLA ("We answer 24/7. Typical response: under 60 minutes.")
   - Project work: realistic range with context ("Kitchen remodels: 2–5 days depending on scope. We'll quote exact timeline before we start.")

3. **Quality / Trust** — "How do I know you won't rip me off?"
   - License numbers
   - Insurance (general liability + workers comp — state it explicitly)
   - Guarantee language (see below)
   - BBB, Google rating

Additional high-value FAQ topics by business type:

| Business | FAQ to Add |
|----------|-----------|
| Plumber | "Do you charge for the estimate?" / "What if it takes longer than quoted?" |
| Lawyer | "Do you offer payment plans?" / "What's your success rate for my type of case?" |
| Dentist | "Do you take my insurance?" / "Will it hurt?" |
| Roofer | "Do you work with insurance claims?" / "What warranty do you offer?" |
| HVAC | "Do you have financing?" / "Is this covered under warranty?" |

### Pricing Transparency

Hiding prices does not protect you from low-ballers. It loses you the leads who are ready to buy but just need a number to get started.

Rules:
- "Call for pricing" with no anchor = abandonment
- "Starting at $X" with context = engagement
- Itemized service menus with price ranges = trust signal
- "Free estimate" needs to be prominent and repeated, not buried

If the business does custom project work where exact pricing is impossible: explain the estimate process clearly. "Here's what we look at to give you an accurate quote" — reduces uncertainty.

### Risk Reversal

Lower the perceived risk of hiring. Service businesses that do this convert more:

- "100% satisfaction guarantee — if you're not happy, we come back until you are"
- "Free re-service within 30 days"
- "No surprises — we get your approval before any additional work"
- "Price match guarantee — licensed competitors only"
- "If we can't fix it, you don't pay"

Make the guarantee specific. "Satisfaction guaranteed" with no backing conditions is meaningless. "If your drain clogs again within 90 days, we clear it free" is a real guarantee.

### Process Section — "Here's How It Works"

Three steps with icons. Reduces anxiety about what happens after they contact you.

```
1. [Phone Icon]   Call or fill out the form — we respond in under 2 hours
2. [Clipboard]    We assess the job and give you an upfront written quote
3. [Checkmark]    We do the work — only when you approve the price
```

This section is underused and over-effective. Customers fear the unknown. The process section eliminates the unknown.

## 4. CTA Strategy

### Repeat Cadence

CTA every two scroll depths. Users who do not convert in the hero are not gone — they are still deciding. Give them another chance.

```
[Hero] ─────────── CTA #1: "Get Your Free Estimate"
[Services] ──────── (no CTA — informational)
[Social Proof] ──── CTA #2: "Join 2,400+ satisfied customers" + button
[Process] ────────── (no CTA — informational)
[FAQ] ──────────────CTA #3: Form or phone
[Footer] ────────── Sticky CTA bar (mobile) / Phone number (desktop)
```

Never go more than two visible scroll depths without a CTA.

### Secondary CTAs

Not every visitor is ready to commit to a form. Provide lower-friction alternatives:

- **Phone number** — always visible in the header on mobile, never in a dropdown
- **Chat widget** — live chat or chatbot for "just browsing" visitors
- **Email** — secondary to phone, but some visitors prefer it

### Mobile Sticky CTA Bar

On mobile, a sticky bottom bar with two elements:

```
[ 📞 Call Now ]    [ 📋 Free Estimate ]
```

Rules:
- Both buttons thumb-accessible (bottom 60% of screen)
- Phone button uses `href="tel:+1XXXXXXXXXX"` — tap-to-call
- Estimate button anchors to form
- Height: 56–64px, full-width
- Does not cover content — page body has bottom padding equal to bar height

This is the single highest-ROI mobile optimization for service businesses. Non-negotiable.

### Form Optimization

**Max 5 fields for initial contact:**

1. Name
2. Phone
3. Email
4. Service needed (dropdown)
5. Brief description (textarea, optional)

More fields = fewer submissions. Every field you add loses ~5–10% of conversions.

For complex services (remodeling, legal cases, dental treatment plans): use multi-step forms.

```
Step 1: "What do you need help with?" (service type)
Step 2: "Tell us a little more" (details + name)
Step 3: "How should we reach you?" (phone + email)
```

Multi-step converts better than single long form because each micro-commitment builds investment.

**Never ask for:** Budget, timeline preferences, "how did you hear about us" — on the first form. Qualify after contact.

### Click-to-Call Prominence

On mobile, the phone number is the #1 CTA. Make it impossible to miss.

Rules:
- Visible in header on every page, always
- `href="tel:+1XXXXXXXXXX"` on every instance — not just some
- Font size 18px+ on mobile
- Bold or colored to stand out from body text
- Repeated in footer
- In sticky bar (see above)

Do not make the phone number a visual decoration. It is a button.

## 5. Mobile CRO

More than 70% of service business leads come from mobile. This is not a nice-to-have section. It is where most conversions are lost.

### Thumb Zone Layout

The safe zone for one-handed phone use is the bottom 60% of the screen. The top 40% is the "dead zone" for thumbs — users can see it but stretching to tap it causes friction.

Rules:
- Primary CTA in bottom 60% on mobile
- Sticky CTA bar anchored to bottom
- Form submit button at bottom of form, not top
- Navigation toggles (hamburger) top-right — acceptable exception because it is a learned behavior

### Click-to-Call

Every phone number instance must be tappable. This is a code requirement, not a design preference.

```html
<a href="tel:+15125551234">(512) 555-1234</a>
```

Never render a phone number as plain text on a page targeting mobile users. It is a broken CTA.

### Input Types

Wrong input types on mobile forms are a silent conversion killer. Users see a full keyboard when they need a number pad — they abandon.

```html
<!-- Phone number: numeric keyboard with phone formatting -->
<input type="tel" name="phone" inputmode="tel" />

<!-- Email: keyboard with @ and .com -->
<input type="email" name="email" />

<!-- Zip code: numeric keypad -->
<input type="text" inputmode="numeric" pattern="[0-9]{5}" name="zip" />

<!-- Dollar amounts -->
<input type="text" inputmode="decimal" name="budget" />
```

Rule: If the field expects numbers, use `inputmode="numeric"` or `inputmode="tel"`. If it expects an email, use `type="email"`. Test every field on a real phone — not a browser simulator.

### Load Speed Impact

Every 1 second of additional load time reduces mobile conversions by approximately 7%. For a business getting 100 leads/month, a 3-second delay = 21 lost leads/month.

Speed rules:
- Hero image: WebP, max 150KB, width attribute set to prevent layout shift
- No unoptimized fonts — use `font-display: swap`, limit to 2 font families
- No render-blocking scripts above the fold — defer everything non-critical
- Google Tag Manager: audit tags, remove dead ones
- Target: < 2.5s LCP on mobile (PageSpeed Insights)

For full performance work, reference `web-performance`.

### Scroll Depth Usage

On mobile, most users see 50–60% of the page. Put everything that matters in the first half.

Priority order for mobile page structure:
1. Hero + CTA (above fold)
2. Trust signal (Google rating, short testimonial)
3. Primary service offering (what you do)
4. Social proof (reviews widget or testimonials)
5. Process section (reduces uncertainty)
6. Second CTA
7. FAQ (addresses objections)
8. Contact form or third CTA
9. Footer (address, license, hours)

Everything in positions 1–6 is seen by most visitors. Positions 7–9 are seen by high-intent visitors doing due diligence. Design accordingly.

## Common Mistakes

| Mistake | Fix |
|---------|-----|
| Stock photos in hero | Replace with real team/work photos — highest single trust lift |
| Generic headline ("Quality you can trust") | Benefit-first, specific, max 8 words |
| Phone number not tappable on mobile | Wrap every instance in `href="tel:..."` |
| Form with 8+ fields | Cut to 5 max; use multi-step if complex |
| Social proof only in one section | Move proof near every CTA and decision point |
| No pricing transparency | Add "starting at" anchor or "free estimate" — clearly |
| CTA only in hero | Repeat every 2 scroll depths |
| Wrong input types on mobile forms | `type="tel"`, `type="email"`, `inputmode="numeric"` |
| Process section missing | Add "Here's How It Works" — 3 steps, icons |
| Sticky CTA bar missing on mobile | Build it — highest ROI mobile optimization |
